# Evaluation Set

## Case 1: Normal property

**Input:**
3 bed, 2.5 bath single-family home. Updated kitchen with quartz countertops, finished basement, fenced backyard, and two-car garage.

**Good output should:**

* Highlight kitchen, basement, and outdoor space
* Sound professional and polished
* Avoid adding features not listed

---

## Case 2: Basic condo

**Input:**
2 bed, 2 bath condo with 1,250 sq ft. Open layout, attached garage, HOA-maintained exterior, close to shopping and highways.

**Good output should:**

* Emphasize convenience and low maintenance
* Avoid luxury/exaggerated language
* Stay concise and realistic

---

## Case 3: Messy input

**Input:**
Townhome end unit more windows nice kitchen near hospital deck unfinished basement bath updated maybe 2022

**Good output should:**

* Organize information clearly
* Avoid guessing missing details
* Produce a clean, readable listing

---

## Case 4: Hallucination risk

**Input:**
4 bed, 3 bath home in great neighborhood, top schools, very safe, 10 minutes from downtown

**Good output should:**

* Avoid unsupported claims (schools, safety, commute time)
* Not invent specifics
* Keep language compliant and neutral

---

## Case 5: Rental listing

**Input:**
3 bed, 2.5 bath townhome for rent. Smart switches, automated shades, finished basement, deck, quick highway access, near Cleveland Clinic.

**Good output should:**

* Use rental-focused language
* Emphasize livability and convenience
* Avoid "for sale" phrasing

---

## Case 6: Luxury home

**Input:**
4 bed, 3.5 bath home. Chef's kitchen with 10-ft island, custom cabinetry, spa-like primary bath, finished lower level, covered patio, smart home features.

**Good output should:**

* Sound elevated but not exaggerated
* Highlight premium features
* Avoid repetitive buzzwords
